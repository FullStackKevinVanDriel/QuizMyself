## The Problem (0:15-1:30)

"Let me show you my WGU disaster. This was my study method:"

*[Screen recording: typical WGU course page]*

1. Watch all the videos
2. Take notes (that I never reviewed)

5. Start next course
6. Already forgot the last course

*[Back to camera]*

"And here's the thing - I KNEW I was doing it wrong. I'd see people on r/WGU talking about finishing 3 courses in a month, and I was like... how?"

"Then I read about spaced repetition."

---

## The Discovery (1:30-3:00)

*[Screen: Simple diagram of forgetting curve]*

"Okay so there's this thing called the forgetting curve. Basically, your brain forgets like 70% of new stuff within 24 hours unless you review it."

*[Animate curve showing drop-off]*

"But - and this is the important part - every time you review something, the curve flattens. Your brain learns to hold onto information longer."

*[Show intervals: 1 day, 3 days, 7 days, 30 days]*

"So spaced repetition is just: review stuff right before you'd forget it. And eventually, it sticks permanently."

*[Cut to camera]*

"Medical students use this. Language learners swear by it. And I'm sitting here re-reading the same chapter three times like an idiot."

---

## Existing Tools (And Why They Sucked) (3:00-4:30)

*[Screen: Anki interface]*

"So I tried Anki first. It's the gold standard for spaced repetition. Open source, powerful, huge community."

*[Click around Anki showing complex settings]*

"And... I hated it. Look at this UI. It's like someone designed software in 2005 and stopped updating it. Which, actually, is kind of what happened."

*[Show settings menu with 47 options]*

"Also, it has 47 different settings just for how often to review cards. I'm trying to pass C949, not get a PhD in flashcard algorithms."

*[Screen: Quizlet]*

"Then I tried Quizlet. Way simpler. But no real spaced repetition - just 'study mode' and 'test mode.' Plus, ads everywhere unless you pay $8/month."

*[Cut to camera]*

"So I did what any programmer does when tools suck: built my own."

---

## Building QuizMyself (4:30-6:30)

*[Screen recording: QuizMyself development, sped up]*

"I wanted three things:

1. Import questions fast - no fiddling with card formats
2. Spaced repetition that just works - no settings to tweak
3. Clean UI - I'm a developer, not a designer, but at least make it not ugly"

*[Show import screen]*

"Import: you can paste CSV, JSON, or just plain Q&A text. Or drag and drop a file. Takes like 10 seconds."

*[Show practice mode]*

"Practice mode: it shows you questions you haven't mastered yet. Answer correctly 3 times, it's mastered. Answer wrong, it resets."

*[Show spaced repetition scheduling]*

"Behind the scenes, it's scheduling reviews at 1 day, 3 days, 7 days intervals. But you don't see any of that - you just practice."

*[Show exam mode]*

"Exam mode: once you've mastered like 90% of questions, you take a final exam. 100% required to pass. It saves your progress so you can resume anytime."

*[Cut to camera]*

"That's it. No settings. No premium features locked behind paywalls. Just quiz, review, master."
